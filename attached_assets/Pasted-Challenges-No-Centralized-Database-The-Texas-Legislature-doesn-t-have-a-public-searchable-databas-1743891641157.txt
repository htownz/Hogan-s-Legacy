Challenges:
No Centralized Database: The Texas Legislature doesn't have a public, searchable database specifically for points of order and their outcomes.
Scattered Information: Points of order information can be in House and Senate journals, committee reports, and sometimes news reports or analyses.
Volume of Data: The legislative sessions involve thousands of bills and resolutions, and there can be many points of order raised throughout the process.
Dynamic Data: Tracking the outcomes of sustained and overruled points of order can require tracking bill amendments, committee changes, and floor action, which can be a lot of dynamic information. 
Here's a general overview of the process and where to look for some of the details:
Finding Information:
Texas Legislature Online (TLO):
You can search for specific bills or resolutions to check the actions taken.
House and Senate Journals: TLO has journals that record the daily proceedings of the House and Senate, which include motions and rulings on points of order.
Amendments: Use TLO to search for amendments that may have resulted from points of order.
Legislative Reference Library of Texas (LRL):
The LRL archives legislative documents, including bill files from various sessions.
Committee Reports:
Look for relevant committee reports for details on amendments made and whether a point of order resulted in the committee modifying a bill.
News Reports and Analyses:
Search through archives of news organizations that cover the Texas Legislature.
Look for publications like the Texas Tribune, Dallas Morning News, and others for summaries or analysis of specific points of order. 
89th Legislative Session House and Senate Rules Analysis
House Rules:
Official Source: The official source for House rules is the House Rules Manual, available on the House website. 
Changes to Committee Structures: It's likely there are changes to committee structures, as is typical from session to session. The number of committees and memberships can vary between legislative sessions. 
Enforcement of Rules: The House Speaker enforces, applies, and interprets the rules. 
Senate Rules:
Official Source: Check the Senate website for the official version of Senate rules. 
General Senate Procedures: The Lieutenant Governor, as President of the Senate, presides over the chamber and enforces the rules. 
General Notes about Texas Legislative Rules:
Adoption of Rules: At the start of each legislative session, the House and Senate adopt rules of procedure. 
Points of Order Enforcement: Points of order are used to point out potential violations of these rules. 
Importance of Rules: Adherence to the rules is critical for ensuring the proper functioning of the legislative process. 
Potential Effects of Sustained Points of Order: These can result in the bill being sent back to committee, an amendment being struck, or causing delays in the process. 
Further Research Suggestions:
Start by focusing on specific bills or issues you're interested in to make the research more manageable.
Contact the Legislative Reference Library or the House Research Organization for more specific information.
